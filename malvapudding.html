MALVA PUDDING

Malva pudding is a delicious dessert with roots in South Africa. Spongy and caramelized, it's filled with apricot jam. It is always served hot with custard and/or ice cream and a cream sauce is drizzled over it right before serving.

Ingredients

  • 3∕4 cup whole milk
  • 1∕2 cup dark brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • tbsp apricot jam, strained
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 1∕2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder

Sauce

  • 1∕2cup heavy cream
  • 1∕2cup whole milk
  • 1 1∕2cup butter
  • 1∕2 cup sugar
  • 1∕4 tsp salt

Method

Step One

Preheat your oven to 180℃ and butter your 20cm square baking dish

Step Two

In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the milk, brown sugar, eggs, apricot jam, melted butter, and vinegar until everything is well mixed.

Step Three

Whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a separate bowl. Mix the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients until everything is well mixed.

Step Four

Pour into the pan and bake for 30‑40 minutes, or until a knife stuck in the middle comes out clean.

Step Five

Just before the pudding is done, make the sauce by putting the cream, milk, butter, sugar, and salt in a pot over medium heat and stirring until the butter melts and the sugar dissolves.

Step Six

When the pudding is done, use a fork to poke holes all over the hot pudding. Then, pour the warm sauce over the pudding. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

Step Seven

Serve hot with a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The pudding can be kept in the fridge for up to 3 days in a sealed container. Reheat the food in a 150℃ oven or in the microwave before serving.
